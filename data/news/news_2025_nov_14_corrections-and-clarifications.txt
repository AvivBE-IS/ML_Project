Corrections and clarifications
------------------------------------------------------------
• An article said Donald Trump had filed a lawsuit in a Florida court against the BBC; however, the president has only threatened legal action (BBC is prepared to make formal apology to Trump to resolve billion-dollar legal threat, 13 November, p7). • An article (Pollock can be catalyst England need for World Cup but maybe someone should have a word, 3 November, p37) referred to “Sir Douglas Jardine”, but the cricketer was not knighted. • A review of Queen Esther said the book opens in the “fictional town of Penacook, New Hampshire”; it is a real place (Back to St Cloud’s, 8 November, Saturday, p54). • Other recently amended articles include: Children being ‘sedated’ by algorithmic YouTube content, MPs hear White House approves increased oil and gas drilling in Alaska’s national wildlife refuge Australians have been promised three free hours of solar power a day.
